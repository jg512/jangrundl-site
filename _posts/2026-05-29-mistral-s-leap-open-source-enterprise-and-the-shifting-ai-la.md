It feels like just yesterday I was reading about a handful of big tech companies pushing the boundaries of what AI could do. OpenAI, Google, Anthropic – they held the spotlight, and their models felt like the only game in town. Now, the scene is changing at warp speed. Every few months, a new player emerges, often from a different corner of the world, bringing fresh ideas and often, a commitment to open-source principles. It's exhilarating to watch, a rapid decentralization of power that wasn't guaranteed.

### The Core of the Summit

I just caught up on the notes from the Mistral AI Now Summit in Paris, and it really brought this feeling home. Mistral AI, a European company, isn't just making noise; they're making waves. The big news centered around a few key announcements, signaling their serious play in the enterprise AI space, while still holding onto their open-source roots for some models.

First up, **Mistral Large**. This is their new flagship model, aiming for the top tier. The claims are impressive: it's supposed to be one of the best reasoning models out there, with strong multilingual capabilities, especially for European languages like French, German, Spanish, and Italian. It handles a hefty 32k token context window, which means it can process a lot of information at once. They're also talking about advanced features like function calling and JSON mode, which are crucial for developers building complex applications.

Then there's **Mistral Small**, a model optimized for balancing performance with cost and speed. The idea here is that not every task needs the full power of a 'Large' model. Small offers a punchy, efficient option that's almost as capable as their popular open-source Mixtral 8x7B model, but tuned for API use. And for text embedding, they introduced **Mistral Embed**, a model designed to convert text into numerical representations for tasks like search and retrieval, also with a respectable 8192 token context window.

Crucially, they're distributing these new models mainly through their API platform, `api.mistral.ai`. While they've built their reputation on models like Mixtral 8x7B and Mistral 7B being open source and downloadable, Mistral Large is currently an API-only offering. This highlights a shift towards a more commercial, enterprise-focused strategy, even as they continue to support the open-source community with other releases.
